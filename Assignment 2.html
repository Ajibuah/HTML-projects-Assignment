A . P . J . Abdul Kalam

Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the Legend

  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avil Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on october 15, 1931, in Rameawaram,Tamil Nadu, India


  • He served as the 11th president of India from 2002 t0 2007.


  • Kalam earned a degree in areonautical engineering from the madras Institute of Technology abd in 1958 joined the Defece Research and Development Oranisation (DRDO)


  • In 1969,he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of thr SLV-III, the first satellite vehicle that was both designed and produced in India


  • Rejoining DRDO in 1982 Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man."


  • Among those successes was Agni, India's first intermediate-range ballistic missle, which incorporated aspects of the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974


  • From 19992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific adviser to the defense minister, and he later served as principal scientific adviser (1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.


  • His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons test solidified India as a nuclear power and established Kalam as a national hero, although the tests caused great concern in the international community.


  • While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 July 2015, aged 83.


  • Wheeler Island, a national missile test site in odisha, was renamed Kalam Island in September 2015.


  • A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015.


  • In Februaury 2018, scientist from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii, in his honour.
